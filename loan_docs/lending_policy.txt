SECTION 1 — SCOPE AND APPLICABILITY
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1.1 This policy applies to all SME credit proposals where:
    - Total borrower group exposure is between ₹25 lakh and ₹25 crore
    - Borrower is a registered Indian entity (Pvt Ltd, LLP, Partnership, Proprietorship)
    - Facility type includes Term Loan, Cash Credit, Letter of Credit, or Bank Guarantee

1.2 Proposals outside these parameters require escalation to:
    - Large Corporate Credit Committee (exposure > ₹25 crore)
    - Micro Credit Team (exposure < ₹25 lakh)

1.3 This policy supersedes ACB-CP-SME-2022 (Version 3.1) in its entirety.

SECTION 2 — ELIGIBLE BORROWERS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

2.1 MINIMUM ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA (all must be met)

    a) Years in operation       : Minimum 3 years from date of incorporation
    b) Audited financials       : Minimum 2 years of audited financial statements
    c) GST registration         : Mandatory for all borrowers above ₹40 lakh turnover
    d) ITR filing               : Minimum 2 years of filed Income Tax Returns
    e) Bureau score (commercial): Minimum 55 / 100 (Experian Commercial Score)
    f) Bureau score (promoter)  : Minimum 650 / 900 (personal CIBIL/Experian score)
    g) No NPA classification    : In any bank or financial institution in last 5 years
    h) No wilful defaulter flag : In RBI CRILC or any credit bureau

2.2 INELIGIBLE BORROWERS (automatic decline — no exceptions)

    a) Borrowers classified as NPA, SMA-2, or written off in last 5 years
    b) Promoters on RBI wilful defaulter list
    c) Entities under IBC / NCLT proceedings
    d) Borrowers with fraud flags in any bureau or bank reference
    e) Shell companies or entities with no demonstrable business operations
    f) Sectors on negative list (Section 8 of this policy)

SECTION 3 — FINANCIAL PARAMETERS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

3.1 DEBT SERVICE COVERAGE RATIO (DSCR)

    Minimum DSCR               : 1.25x (based on average of last 2 years)
    Preferred DSCR             : 1.50x and above
    DSCR below 1.25x           : Automatic condition — additional collateral required
    DSCR below 1.00x           : Hard stop — proposal not eligible for sanction

    DSCR Calculation:
    DSCR = (Net Profit After Tax + Depreciation + Interest) / (Annual Debt Repayment + Interest)

    Note: DSCR must be calculated using AUDITED financials, not borrower-stated figures.
    Where audited and stated figures differ by more than 5%, audited figures must be used.

3.2 LEVERAGE RATIOS

    Maximum Debt/Equity ratio  : 3.0x
    Preferred Debt/Equity      : Below 2.0x
    Maximum Total Outside Liabilities to Net Worth (TOL/NW): 4.0x

3.3 PROFITABILITY

    Minimum Net Profit Margin  : 5% (average of last 2 years)
    Declining margins          : If margin has declined more than 3 percentage points
                                  YoY, mandatory commentary required in credit memo
    Loss-making entity         : Not eligible unless prior 2 years were profitable
                                  and current year loss is demonstrably one-time

3.4 CURRENT RATIO

    Minimum Current Ratio      : 1.20x
    Below 1.00x                : Hard stop for working capital facilities

3.5 INTEREST COVERAGE RATIO

    Minimum ICR                : 2.0x
    Below 1.5x                 : Mandatory escalation to senior credit officer

SECTION 4 — EXPOSURE NORMS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

4.1 SINGLE BORROWER LIMITS

    Maximum exposure — unsecured   : ₹50 lakh
    Maximum exposure — secured     : ₹25 crore (within SME policy)
    Exposure above ₹5 crore        : Mandatory credit committee approval
    Exposure above ₹10 crore       : Board credit committee approval required

4.2 LOAN-TO-VALUE (LTV) RATIOS

    Residential property           : Maximum 75% LTV
    Commercial / Industrial property: Maximum 65% LTV
    Plant and machinery            : Maximum 60% of depreciated book value
                                     or 50% of market value, whichever is lower
    Stock and receivables          : Maximum 75% of book value (for CC limit)
    Quoted shares                  : Maximum 50% of 6-month average market price

4.3 TENURE LIMITS

    Term loan — plant and machinery: Maximum 84 months (7 years)
    Term loan — other purposes     : Maximum 60 months (5 years)
    Cash credit / OD               : 12 months — annual renewal
    Letter of credit               : Maximum 180 days usance

4.4 PROMOTER CONTRIBUTION

    Minimum promoter contribution  : 25% of project cost for new projects
    Existing business expansion    : Assessed case by case — minimum 20%

SECTION 5 — CUSTOMER CONCENTRATION RISK
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

5.1 DEFINITION
    Customer concentration is defined as the percentage of total revenue
    attributable to the top 1, 2, or 3 customers of the borrower.

5.2 THRESHOLDS

    Single customer > 40% of revenue    : CONDITION — borrower must provide
                                           written confirmation of relationship
                                           longevity and purchase order pipeline

    Top 2 customers > 60% of revenue    : CONDITION — borrower must provide
                                           a written diversification plan with
                                           timeline and milestones. Review at
                                           12 months mandatory.

    Top 2 customers > 75% of revenue    : HARD STOP for facilities above ₹1 crore
                                           unless 3+ year contracts in place with
                                           creditworthy counterparties

    Export concentration (single country > 70%): Additional country risk assessment
                                           mandatory. Hedging policy required.

5.3 MITIGANTS THAT CAN OFFSET CONCENTRATION RISK
    - Long-term purchase contracts (3+ years) with creditworthy buyers
    - Buyer credit insurance / export credit guarantee
    - Demonstrated history of 5+ years with the same customer
    - Active pipeline of new customer acquisition (evidenced)


════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 6 — COLLATERAL REQUIREMENTS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

6.1 COLLATERAL COVERAGE RATIO
    Minimum collateral coverage    : 1.25x of total exposure
    Preferred collateral coverage  : 1.50x and above

6.2 VALUATION REQUIREMENTS
    - All immovable property must be valued by an RBI-empanelled valuer
    - Valuation must not be older than 6 months at time of sanction
    - Plant and machinery: CA/engineer certificate of current value required
    - Revaluation mandatory every 3 years during loan tenure

6.3 EXISTING CHARGES
    - Where property is already mortgaged, pari passu or second charge
      requires prior lender NOC
    - No second charge without first lender NOC for exposures above ₹2 crore

6.4 PERSONAL GUARANTEES
    - Mandatory from all promoters holding above 10% shareholding
    - Net worth of guarantors must be minimum 1.5x of total exposure
    - Joint and several guarantee required where multiple promoters

SECTION 7 — BUREAU AND FRAUD CHECKS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

7.1 MANDATORY BUREAU CHECKS
    - Commercial bureau report (Experian/CIBIL): Mandatory
    - Promoter personal bureau (all promoters > 10% shareholding): Mandatory
    - CERSAI search: Mandatory for all secured facilities
    - MCA21 / ROC search: Mandatory for private limited companies
    - CRILC check: Mandatory

7.2 DPD INTERPRETATION
    - 0 DPD in last 24 months           : Satisfactory
    - 1 instance of 30 DPD in 24 months : Acceptable with explanation
    - 2+ instances of 30 DPD            : Condition — restructuring history check
    - Any 60+ DPD in last 36 months     : Hard stop
    - Any 90+ DPD (NPA) in last 60 months: Hard stop

7.3 UNANSWERED BUREAU ENQUIRIES
    - Enquiries without sanctions in last 6 months: Explanation mandatory
    - 3+ unanswered enquiries in 12 months: Escalation to fraud desk required
    - Pattern of enquiries coinciding with financial stress: Red flag — enhanced DD

7.4 FRAUD INDICATORS — MANDATORY INVESTIGATION
    The following require mandatory referral to the fraud desk:
    a) Revenue overstated by more than 10% vs GST/ITR
    b) CC cycling (full repayment and same-day redrawdown) within 30 days
       of loan application
    c) Bank turnover more than 30% below stated figure in application
    d) Round-number transactions inconsistent with business profile
    e) Multiple lender enquiries without sanctions (possible loan stacking)

SECTION 8 — SECTOR POLICY
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

8.1 PREFERRED SECTORS (enhanced limits applicable)
    - IT/ITES and technology services
    - Healthcare and pharmaceuticals
    - Food processing and agro-processing
    - Renewable energy (solar, wind)
    - Export-oriented manufacturing (subject to concentration check)

8.2 STANDARD SECTORS (normal policy applies)
    - Textile manufacturing and export      ← APPLICABLE TO THIS CASE
    - Engineering and auto components
    - Construction materials
    - Retail and distribution
    - Education and training

8.3 CAUTION SECTORS (enhanced monitoring, lower LTV)
    - Real estate development
    - Hospitality and hotels
    - Gems and jewellery
    - Media and entertainment

8.4 NEGATIVE LIST (not eligible — no exceptions)
    - Tobacco and tobacco products
    - Gambling and betting
    - Speculative trading (commodities, forex, crypto)
    - Arms and ammunition
    - Entities under FEMA violation investigation

SECTION 9 — CONTINGENT LIABILITIES
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

9.1 All contingent liabilities must be disclosed by the borrower
    and captured in the credit appraisal.

9.2 TREATMENT OF CONTINGENT LIABILITIES
    - Export obligations (EPCG, Advance Licence): Must be stated in memo.
      If obligation is more than 25% of annual revenue, stress test required.
    - Guarantees given by borrower to third parties: Included in group exposure
    - Tax demands under appeal: Noted; provision assessment required if > ₹10 lakh
    - Legal disputes: Law firm opinion required if claim > ₹25 lakh

9.3 UNDISCLOSED CONTINGENT LIABILITIES
    If discovered during due diligence that borrower failed to disclose
    a material contingent liability, the proposal must be escalated to
    the credit committee regardless of the liability amount.
    This constitutes a negative character assessment of the promoter.

⚠ NOTE ON EPCG OBLIGATIONS:
    Export Promotion Capital Goods (EPCG) obligations represent a future
    export commitment. Failure to meet these results in customs duty
    recovery plus interest. For textile exporters, EPCG obligations
    must always be captured and stress-tested.

SECTION 10 — DOCUMENTATION CHECKLIST
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

10.1 MANDATORY PRE-SANCTION DOCUMENTS
    □ Loan application form (bank format)
    □ KYC — entity (Certificate of Incorporation, PAN, GST)
    □ KYC — promoters (PAN, Aadhaar, photographs)
    □ Audited financial statements — last 3 years
    □ ITR with computation — last 3 years
    □ GST returns — last 4 quarters
    □ Bank statements — last 12 months (all banks)
    □ Bureau reports — entity and all promoters
    □ Property valuation report (RBI empanelled valuer)
    □ Search report — title clear certificate
    □ CERSAI search report
    □ MCA21 / ROC extract
    □ List of directors and shareholding pattern
    □ Projected financials — next 2 years (for term loans)
    □ Sanction letters from existing lenders
    □ Stock and debtor statement (for CC limits)
    □ Personal net worth statement — all promoters

10.2 SECTOR-SPECIFIC DOCUMENTS (textile exporters)
    □ Export orders / purchase contracts from buyers
    □ DGFT registration and IEC code
    □ EPCG licence details and outstanding obligation
    □ Forward contract / hedging details (if applicable)
    □ Buyer creditworthiness — last 2 years payment history


════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 11 — CREDIT MEMO REQUIREMENTS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

11.1 Every credit proposal must include a credit memo covering:
    a) Executive summary — recommendation and key rationale
    b) Borrower background and management assessment
    c) Financial analysis with ratio table
    d) Banking conduct assessment
    e) Bureau assessment
    f) Risk assessment — minimum 3 key risks with mitigants
    g) Collateral assessment with LTV calculation
    h) Policy compliance checklist
    i) Conditions and covenants recommended
    j) Monitoring triggers — events that require review

11.2 MANDATORY COVENANTS FOR SME TERM LOANS
    - Annual submission of audited financials within 6 months of year end
    - Quarterly submission of stock and debtor statements
    - DSCR maintenance covenant — annual review
    - Prior approval for additional borrowing above ₹50 lakh
    - Prior approval for promoter dividend above 30% of PAT
    - Insurance of all assets charged to the bank
